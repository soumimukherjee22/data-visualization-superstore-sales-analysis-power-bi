<h2><a class="anchor" id="project-overview"></a>Project Overview</h2>

This Power BI dashboard analyses \*\*Superstore sales performance\*\* to uncover insights on sales, profit, and customer behaviour.  It helps identify top-performing regions, product categories, and time periods, enabling data-driven decision-making.

---
<h2><a class="anchor" id="business-problem-statement"></a>Business Problem Statement</h2>

The Superstore company operates across multiple regions, offering products in various categories such as Technology, Furniture, and Office Supplies. Despite achieving consistent sales growth, the management lacks a comprehensive view of sales performance and profitability across regions, customer segments, and time periods.

Due to fragmented data and manual reporting, decision-makers struggle to identify:
- Which regions and product categories drive the most revenue and profit
- Where discounts are negatively impacting profit margins
- How seasonal trends affect sales performance
- Which customer segments are the most valuable to target for future growth

This lack of data-driven insight limits the company’s ability to make strategic business decisions, optimise marketing efforts, and allocate resources effectively.

---
<h2><a class="anchor" id="business-goal"></a>Business Goal</h2>

To build an interactive Power BI dashboard that provides a 360° view of sales performance, enabling the management to:
- Monitor key performance indicators (KPIs) such as Sales, Profit, Discount, and Quantity
- Identify high-performing regions, products, and customer segments
- Understand seasonal and category-wise trends
- Support data-driven decision-making to improve overall profitability and operational efficiency

<h2><a class="anchor" id="key-insights"></a>Key Insights</h2>

- **West region** achieved the highest sales, while **Central region** showed areas for improvement.
- **Office Supplies** generated the most sales volume.
- **Technology category** provided the highest profit margins.
- **November–December** were the top-performing months in sales.
